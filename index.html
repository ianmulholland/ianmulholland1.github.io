Ian Mulholland

Student @ Berkeley; Baseball Card Enthusiast; Technology & Development



As a 7 year old tearing through packs of cards, I obsessed over completing sets of players and teams. I see the rich stories that baseball cards can tell of players, teams, and eras. The basic and advanced statistics on the back, the player photograph on the front, and the short biography at the top of the card all lead to important insights about a player or a particular decade of baseball. Having worked in India at a grassroots organization for a number of years and at MIT's flagship organization for social impact entrepreneurs, I learned how important quantitative metrics and the user centered design of systems were in improving the impact of community development initiatives. I am interested in building data and information systems that will allow social impact organizations to make better decisions and deliver more effective solutions. I am particularly interested in the role of data analytics and information architecture in the development sector.
